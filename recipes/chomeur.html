Description:

Pouding ch么meur otherwise known as the "The poor man's pudding" is a dessert that was created during the early years of the great depression in Quebec, Canada. Today, it is casually served as a regional dessert, perhaps being a bit more popular during the saison des sucres, when maple sap is collected and processed and is usually part of the offerings during a meal at a sugar shack, but it is not specifically a maple dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 1½ cups All-Purpose Flour.
  • 1 tsp Baking Powder.
  • 1 Egg.
  • 1 cup White Sugar.
  • ¼ cup Margarine.
  • 1 cup Milk.
  • 2 cups Water.
  • 2 cups Brown Sugar.
  • ¼ cup Margarine. (for sauce)
  • ¼ tsp Vanilla Extract.

Directions:

  1. Preheat an oven to 325°F (165°C). Grease a 9x13inch baking dish.

  2. Sift the flour and baking powder together in a small bowl. Beat the egg, sugar, and ¼ cup margarine together in a large bowl. Add the flour mixture alternately with the milk to the egg mixture, stirring just to combine. Pour the batter into prepared dish.

  3. Bring the water to a boil in a saucepan; stir in the brown sugar, ¼ cup margarine, and vanilla extract into the water and return to a boil for 2 minutes. Pour the sauce over the batter.

  4. Bake in a preheated oven until the center is set, about 45 minutes. Best served warm.
