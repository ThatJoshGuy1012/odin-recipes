Tourtière

Description:

Tourtière is a French Canadian meat pie dish originating from the province of Quebec. usually made with minced pork, veal or beef and potatoes. Wild game is sometimes used. Tourtière is not exclusive to Quebec. It is a traditional French-Canadian dish served throughout Canada and the bordering areas of the United States.

Crust

  • 4½ cups all-purpose flour.
  • 1½ tsp kosher salt.
  • 1½ cups unsalted butter, sliced, frozen.
  • ½ cup and 2½ tbsp ice cold water.
  • 1 tbsp Distilled white vinegar.

Spice blend

  • 1 tbsp kosher salt.
  • ½ tbsp freshly ground black pepper.
  • ½ tbsp dried thyme.
  • ¾ tsp dried sage.
  • ¾ tsp ground cinnamon.
  • ¾ tsp ground ginger.
  • ⅜ tsp freshly grated nutmeg.
  • ⅜ tsp ground allspice.
  • ⅜ tsp ground mustard.
  • ⅙ tsp ground cloves.
  • 1½ pinch cayenne pepper.

Filling

  • 1½ large russet potato, peeled and quartered.
  • ½ tbsp kosher salt.
  • 1½ tbsp butter.
  • 1½ large onion, finely chopped.
  • 1½ pinch salt.
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed.
  • ¾ finely diced celery.
  • 1½ pound ground pork.
  • 1½ pound ground beef.
  • 1 cup potato cooking water, plus more as needed.

Egg Wash

  • 1½ large egg.
  • 1½ tbsp water.

Directions:

  1. Place flour,salt, and frozen butter slices into the bowl of a food processor. Pulse on and off until the butter is about the size of pease, about 30 seconds. Stir vinegar into cold water; drizzle water/vinegar into the flour mixture. Pulse on and off until mixture is crumbly and holds together when you pinch a piece off, about 10 seconds. If dough isn't pressing together, drizzle in another tsp of water.

  2. Transfer mixture to a work surface. Press it together until it becomes a lump of dough. Wrap in plastic wrap. Refrigerate until chilled, at least 1 hour.

  3. Mis salt, pepper, thyme, sage, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, allspice, mustard, clove and cayenne together in a small bowl.

  4. Place potato quarters in a saucepan; cover with cold water. Add 1 tsp kosher salt. Bring to a boil over high heat; reduce heat. Simmer until cooked through, 10 to 15 minutes. Scoop out potatoes and transfer to a bowl; save cooking liquid. Mash potatoes with a potato masher.

  5. Melt butter in a skilled over medium heat. Add chopped onion and a pinch of salt. Cook and stir until onions turn golden, 10 to 15 minutes.

  6. Stir garlic, celery and spice blend into the skillet with onions. Stir until onion mixture is evenly coated with the spices, about 30 seconds. Add ground beef and ground pork. Ladle about ¾ cup of the potato cooking liquid into the skillet.

  7. Cook and stir until meat is browned and has a very fine, almost pastelike texture. Continue cooking, stirring occasionally, until meat is tender and most of the liquid has evaporated, about 45 minutes. Stir in mashed potatoes. Remove from heat and cool to room temperature.

  8. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
